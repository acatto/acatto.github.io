Poetry

I mostly write haiku, thought I've been branching out and experimenting with new forms. Here are some of the pieces I've written.

Haiku

Vacuous echoes
Churn bountiful broken gears
In the lonesome mind

Riding manic waves
Probabilities low now
Many worlds collapse

Waves, washing ashore
Foaming probabilities
Frame the multiverse

Out in pouring rain
Each drop bears timeless stories
Reincarnate flow

Our fingertips slip
The piper calls you up now
I'm stuck in his draft

Several syllables
Constricting prison for some
Wonderland for me

Curtains draw slowly
Projecting worlds I once had
Into quotient spaces

Grave words strike softly
To be etched on our tombstone
We're buried alive

Starry, starry night
Words from the mouth of my youth
Must Don a new Dawn

She draws me in close
Soul deep, dark, enigmatic
Yet eyes crystalline

Alas, time grows short
The Mute voices yell softly
In vast Empty space

Rhythmic Poems

The thoughts inside the formalist's mind
might sound quite pedantic
but who says all that of the rational kind
cannot be romantic?

That overwhelming urge to systematize
might sound quite pedantic
yet it does not imply that behind these eyes
lies grey matter unromantic

Encoding each and every line
might sound quite pedantic
but emerging from these symbolic signs
something wonderfully romantic

so laugh, so love, be free, be kind!
don't be too pedantic!
for rationality is blind
if it's not romantic!


"Stochastic Winds"

Towards me, eerie stochastic winds blow
Strange waves, on which a bottle aglow
Contains ink-soaked parchment, with my fate astow
Line by line, from it I read
Repeating the one thought I’ll come to know
That I’d never be free

Euphoria strikes me from the high tide
Knowing the moon shines bright on my side
Surfing manic waves I’d forever ride
I cry out songs, ecstatic with glee
The idea could never come to mind
That still, I am not free

The low tide took me by surprise
Yet I was submerged, the sea stings my eyes
It seeps into my ears, this ocean of lies
Why has the moon turned against me?
It was then that I’d finally internalize
That I’d never be free

Up rose a tsunami, guided by divine hands
It made me believe I was more than a man
Prophetic delusions, on which I now stand
Modality, slowly becoming reality
The voices whisper “it’s all part of their plan”
I can’t, I won’t be free

This exorcism I know too well
Approaching near the gates of hell
Past the horizon, to singularity I fell
Too weak to scream, I follow cruel destiny
“Draw me out of this morbid spell!”
For alas, I am not free

Prose Poems

"Abstract Pulsations"

You are the abstract pulsations
oscillating the fibers of my ego
as if thrusting this inexplicable dualism through a compounded product space
projecting away the awaiting componential singular doom
and ourselves into a quotient space
in which our atemporal paths shall reside;
our images may be projected into real space
but only locally
as our modes are manifold
yet substantially unified
